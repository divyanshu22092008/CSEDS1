    Theorem-
    History-
    Pythagoras of Samos was an ancient Ionian Greek philosopher and the eponymous founder of Pythagoreanism. He was born around 570 BC on the island of Samos, off the coast of modern-day Turkey. Pythagoras made significant contributions to mathematics, music, and philosophy. He is best known for the Pythagorean theorem, which relates to the lengths of the sides of a right triangle. Pythagoras and his followers believed in the concept of numbers as the fundamental principles of the universe, and they explored mathematical relationships in various aspects of life, including geometry and music theory.

    Formulas-

    In a right-angled triangle with sides 'a' and 'b', and hypotenuse 'c':
    • c² = a² + b²
    • a = √(c² - b²)
    • b = √(c² - a²)
      where
    • 'c' is the length of the hypotenuse
    • 'a' and 'b' are the lengths of the other two sides
